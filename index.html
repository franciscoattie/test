Five Largest Protests in US History

The tradition of protests that demand progressive change in the United States continues.

Here are the country's top five protests in terms of attendance:

  1. George Floyd protests. Nationwide, 2020, with an estimated 26 million protesters;
  2. Women's March. Nationwide, 2017, drew an estimated 5.6 million people;
  3. March for Our Lives. 2018, a student-led protest against gun violence, drew about 2 million people;
  4. Women's March. 2018, drew about 1.5 million protesters;
  5. March on Washington for Lesbian, Gay and Bi Equal Rights and Liberation. 1993, drew about 1 million people.
